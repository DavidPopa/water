# Second-Order Thinking

Trace the full consequence chain of: **$ARGUMENTS**

Most people stop at first-order effects. Go deeper.

## Process

1. **First-order effects** (immediate, obvious):
   - What happens right away?
   - Who is directly affected?
   - What changes immediately?

2. **Second-order effects** (consequences of consequences):
   - What do the first-order effects cause?
   - How do affected parties respond or adapt?
   - What incentives shift?

3. **Third-order effects** (the ripple):
   - What feedback loops emerge?
   - What unintended consequences appear?
   - What becomes easier or harder as a result?

## Consequence Map

| Order | Effect | Positive/Negative | Timeframe |
|-------|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| 1st | ... | +/- | Immediate |
| 2nd | ... | +/- | Weeks/Months |
| 3rd | ... | +/- | Months/Years |

## Hidden Costs and Benefits

- **Hidden costs**: What looks free now but has a delayed price?
- **Hidden benefits**: What looks costly now but pays off later?
- **Feedback loops**: Does any effect amplify or dampen itself over time?

## Output

- Full consequence chain (3 orders deep)
- Identified hidden costs and unintended consequences
- Recommendation: proceed, modify, or reconsider — based on the FULL picture, not just first-order effects
