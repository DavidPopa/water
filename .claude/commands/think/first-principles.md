# First Principles Thinking

Deconstruct **$ARGUMENTS** to its fundamental truths, then rebuild from the ground up.

## Process

1. **State the current approach**: What is the conventional wisdom or existing solution?

2. **List all assumptions**: What beliefs, conventions, or "best practices" underlie this approach?

3. **Challenge each assumption**:

| Assumption | Truly fundamental? | Evidence | Verdict |
|------------|-------------------|----------|---------|
| ... | Yes/No | ... | Keep/Discard |

4. **Identify base truths**: What remains after stripping away assumptions? These are the non-negotiable constraints and physical/logical laws.

5. **Rebuild from truths**: Starting ONLY from the base truths, what solution emerges? Do not reference the original approach — build fresh.

6. **Compare approaches**:
   - Original approach vs. first-principles approach
   - Where do they align? Where do they diverge?
   - What does the first-principles version do better?

## Output

- List of base truths (the real constraints)
- A rebuilt solution constructed only from those truths
- Concrete recommendation: keep the original, adopt the new, or hybrid
