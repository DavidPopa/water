# Opportunity Cost Analysis

Evaluate the true cost of choosing: **$ARGUMENTS**

Every "yes" is a "no" to something else. What are you giving up?

## Process

1. **State the proposed choice**: What are you leaning toward?

2. **List all alternatives**: What else could you do with the same time, money, or resources?

3. **Compare gains and losses**:

| Option | What You Gain | What You Lose | Time Cost | Reversible? |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| Chosen path | ... | ... | ... | Yes/No |
| Alternative A | ... | ... | ... | Yes/No |
| Alternative B | ... | ... | ... | Yes/No |
| Do nothing | ... | ... | None | Yes |

4. **Identify the best alternative you are NOT choosing**: This is your true opportunity cost. Not the worst option — the BEST one you are walking away from.

5. **The key question**: Is the chosen path worth MORE than the best alternative? Be honest.

6. **Reversibility check**: If the choice is easily reversible, the opportunity cost is lower. If it is a one-way door, the cost is higher and deserves more scrutiny.

## Output

- Clear comparison table of all options
- The true opportunity cost (best forgone alternative)
- Verdict: proceed with chosen path, switch to an alternative, or gather more information before deciding
