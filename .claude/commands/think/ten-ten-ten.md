# 10/10/10 Rule

Evaluate **$ARGUMENTS** across three time horizons to cut through short-term emotion.

## Process

### 10 Minutes from Now
- How will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes?
- What emotions are driving you right now? (fear, excitement, frustration, pressure)
- Are those emotions reliable guides or temporary noise?

### 10 Months from Now
- How will you feel about this decision in 10 months?
- Will it matter? Will you remember the short-term discomfort?
- What will the situation look like after the dust settles?

### 10 Years from Now
- How will you feel about this decision in 10 years?
- Does it align with your long-term direction?
- Will you regret doing it — or regret NOT doing it?

## Analysis

- **If all three align**: Decision is clear. Act with confidence.
- **If short-term pain but long-term gain**: Do it. Short-term discomfort fades.
- **If short-term gain but long-term regret**: Do not do it. You are trading your future for comfort now.
- **If uncertain at all horizons**: You need more information, not more deliberation.

## Output

- Assessment at each time horizon
- Whether short-term emotions are helping or misleading
- Clear recommendation: act, wait, or reject
