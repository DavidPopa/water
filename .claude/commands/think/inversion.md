# Inversion Thinking

Instead of asking "how do I succeed at $ARGUMENTS?", ask "how would I guarantee failure?"

## Process

1. **State the goal**: What does success look like?

2. **Invert — list all failure modes**: How could this absolutely, certainly fail?

| # | Way to Fail | Likelihood | Impact |
|---|-------------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | ... | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... |

3. **For each failure mode**: What specific action prevents it?

| Failure Mode | Prevention Action |
|-------------|------------------|
| ... | ... |

4. **Flip the prevention list**: This IS your success plan. The actions that prevent failure are the actions that enable success.

5. **Prioritize by risk**: Sort prevention actions by (likelihood x impact) of the failure they prevent. High-risk failures get addressed first.

## Output

- Ranked list of failure modes with likelihood and impact
- A concrete action plan derived from inverting those failures
- Top 3 actions to take immediately
